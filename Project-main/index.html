Speculation

The purpose of my project is to allow users to express creativity through collaging. The tool should let users create their own unique composition based on a set assortment of images and icons, as well as a large canvas space. The values that guide my project are accessibility and creative expression, which won’t require prior drawing skills. My tool will lack the requirement for users to draw their own image and allow them to freely experiment with composition. The constraints that I will put in place are the lack of a pen tool to force users away from drawing to focus on composition, and utilising what is given within the tool.

As per the Expression and Information design experiment, users will have control over where they decide to place each image and how they would like to arrange and transform each image (scaling, rotating, image layering, multiple of the same image). Audio and visual feedback may be included to provide a more obvious response to user interaction. For example, sound queues when an image is selected, picked up or dropped, and the darkening of buttons when they have been clicked. The tool will have the inclusion of buttons and sliders to perform different functions, such as adjusting the layering of images, rotating, scaling, as well as an undo and redo button.

Research - Provided Tools

I chose Concrete Nest by Rianna Suen & Struan Fraser as my first provided tool to research. The concept of the word collage is what I found most enjoyable, and I based my initial project idea on the concept of a tool that provides users with existing sprites, which they can arrange in the “nest” (the allocated canvas space that I also want to implement in my project).

The second provided research tool that I researched was the tile-flipping toy by Ash K. I enjoyed the different buttons to transform and customise the outcome, and also aim to implement those in my tool. However, the main component that stuck out to me was the “What?” button, which provided users with guidance on what each button did.

Research - Independently Researched Tools

Bird Bakery by Sebastian Scaini & Del Nordlund is a character-building tool that provides users with different options for each part of the bird and allows for different combinations of parts. The end goal of the tool is for users to generate their own unique bird based on the set options of the tool. I found that despite the seemingly closed constraints and simple concept, the comments were filled with many unique birds, with users expressing their own creativity and style in spite of not directly designing each part themselves. The tool is also highly accessible as it exclusively requires users to click on the icons at the top, excluding the use of keyboard shortcuts or extended mouse clicks.

Under Construction by Wes is a limited art tool that utilises different coloured layers and a cut-out feature that users can freely outline the shape using their mouse. Each layer is a different colour that creates contrast between each cut-out layer. The tool also includes a small bar at the bottom of the canvas where cutouts created by the user can be dragged and dropped into the canvas. Despite the straightforward concept, I discovered that the constraint of having to cut inside a previous cut area to reach the next layer was quite interesting, especially when attempting to create a more intricate design. Prompting users to be creative and considerate of where they were making cuts.

Conceptual response and Technical approach

My tool is based on the concept of information design in the context of collage. After consideration, I have made the changes to allow users to upload their own images to encourage more continuous use over time. I have kept the initial concept of providing users with a set of stickers to drag and drop onto the canvas, and the lack of a pen tool to encourage users to think more creatively within the scope of collaging. By allowing users to upload their own images, it encourages them to experiment with how they can create something unique on different images using the same stickers.

The image upload feature was a relatively new implementation. Initially, my concept was to give users a set selection of background images. After some discussion and feedback from peers and my target audience, I found that users are more likely to revisit and keep the tool in mind for long term use if they are able to upload their own images. The uploaded image would be set as the background layer.

Previously, I had considered using sliders to transform the stickers. In developing my prototype, I found that it made more sense to manipulate the stickers directly using a bounding box. This was done using a Konva transformer.

Peer user testing form reflection

“Creative tool name suggestions”

In this question, I had asked for name suggestions as I had yet to come up with a name for my creative tool. The general theme within the responses was very cute names that were based on the vibes of the stickers. Based on the responses, I have decided to go with the name “Fuwakura”, inspired by the Japanese purikura photobooth that also features stickers and image effects.

“What other kinds of stickers would you like to see in the tool?”

I wanted to ask testers for input on what other stickers they would like to see. Most answers encouraged me to continue with the cute and playful theme that I currently have in my creative tool. Things such as accessories and mouth expressions were the most recurring responses.

“What additional features would you like to see?”

The responses in this question highlighted the user's desire to have an effect that would change the background image that users uploaded, as well as stickers that they could place themselves. I plan to add overlays and potentially filters that change the uploaded image. (Borders, image filters, particle effects)

“How easy did you find the tool to use?”

I wanted to get feedback on the learnability of my tool and how easy users found the tool to use. 50% of users rated the tool a 4/5 for ease of use, with 38% giving it 5/5 and only 13% recording a score of 3/5. When developing my tool further, I will add an instructional pop up to clearly guide users on what the tool's functions are.

“Using the scale, rate how restrictive you found the tool.”

As one of my main focuses for my tool was accessibility and the ability to freely create using collage, I wanted to see how restrictive users found the tool. The responses were unsurprising, as I knew that having a limited selection of stickers would limit how much freedom users had to create something unique. The average rating for restrictiveness was 3.56/5. Once I implement more stickers and the additional feature highlighted in question 2, I am hopeful that users will feel they have a bit more freedom in using my tool.

“3 words you would use to describe my tool.”

I wanted to record the overall vibe and a simple indication of how users felt about my creative tool. The most common answers were “cute”, “fun” and “simple”. These gave me an insight into what kind of stylistic choices I can make with the design of the site and what would seem cohesive with the stickers I currently have.

“Please identify the main concept of my tool.”

The majority of users were able to identify that collaging was the main creative context of my tool, this inidates that the creative context that I wanted to communicate was well received.

“Could you see yourself revisiting the creative tool?”

As my tool is intended for continuous use over time, I wanted to record the likelihood of users revisiting my site and continuing to use the creative tool. 31% of users said yes, and 63% expressed that maybe they would. Ideally, when a wider variety of stickers and overlays is added, users will be more inclined to reuse the tool.

“Did you feel that you needed prior knowledge of collaging to use my tool?”

Due to accessibility being one of the key focuses in my concept, I wanted feedback on whether users felt that they needed prior experience with collaging to be able to use my tool successfully. 94% of users stated that they felt they did not need prior knowledge of collaging.

“Other comments, questions or thoughts?”

Text response for additional comments and suggestions. The responses for this question highlighted some technical and user issues with my tool. One of the most prominent issues is the collision box of the stickers, as users commented that it was easy to select the wrong sticker and that there was a large amount of empty space around the sticker that would register as clicking on the sticker. The general theme of the responses addressed potential quality of life actions, such as deleting assets and copy/pasting

“Placement of the stickers might need to be higher on the page? I didn't think about that it was intended for a face pic until I had uploaded my landscape then looked for the stickers” This response prompted me to think deeper about the placement of the sticker bar. In further development, I will aim to place the stickers in a more easily spotted area.
